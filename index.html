How we scored

Categories

  • Highlights (×2.5): Activities for all four of us — adult interests primary, since the kids are happy with anything new and tolerable at this age
  • Stress / hassle (×2.0): Flights, driving, walkability, jet lag, language, food, crowds, and hotel transitions (fewer moves = less stress)
  • Show-stopper risks (×2.0): Trip-canceling events only — hurricanes, wildfires, heat waves, volcanic. Below 4 only if >10% probability of significant disruption. Routine hazards (drownings, medical gaps) listed for awareness but don't affect the score.
  • Weather fit (×1.0): vs. 82°F ceiling and toddler comfort
  • Cost (×1.0): 14-night total for a family of four
  • Right-age fit (×1.0): Does this destination work well at ages 2 + 5, or is it meaningfully better at 8–12?
  • Accommodations (×1.0): Availability of clean, modern, family-friendly lodging

Profile assumptions

  • Love hiking, scenery, culture, history, walking urban + nature
  • Not big beach/pool people — OK as a component, not the point
  • Genuinely relaxing with young kids — minimize child stress
  • Ideal 50/50 urban/nature; tolerate 70/30 in either direction
  • 82°F (28°C) is pushing our heat tolerance limit
  • Fewer hotel transitions matters — short stays are stressful
  • Low tolerance for dated, buggy, or worn accommodations
